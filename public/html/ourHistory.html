House of Hashimoto

Our History

1972

House of Hashimoto was originally founded by Japan immigrant Hiromori Hashimoto, and his wife, Manami. The two opened their first restaurant in the heart of Wake Forest, NC, at the intersection of Rogers Rd. and Forestville Rd., then called "Hashi's Diner."

1973

Almost going out of business in their first year, the Hashimotos persevered and began developing their roots. Originallu, items on the menu focused on popular foods such udon and uramaki, advertised as "noodles" and "inside-out sushi."

1975 - 1983

With steady success in their humble restaurant, the couple began to grow their family, and raised Fuyuki, Akiyo, Goro, Banri, and Asao.

1990s

The children became integral components of Hashi's Diner, and eventually came to manage the entire operation. With Fuyuki's vision and leadership, she and all of her siblings saved most of their earnings with the hope to one day expand on their parents' dreams in coming to the U.S. The children meticulously planned to grow the business, expanding their menu items to integrate more authentic Japanese flavors.

2004

A major shift occurred. The Hashimoto family pivotted their business to invite a larger audience, while also embracing their Japanese heritage. They closed down Hashi's Diner and set a new location, right in the heart of North Carolina. The menu was upgraded to cater an audience with a refined pallete and intrigue for authentic Japanese cuisine. And while Hiromori and Manami passed away before they could witness the fruits of their children's labor, they are forever remembered. Namely, with the new location, the business was renamed House of Hashimoto in honor of Hiromori and Manami.

Today

Right in the middle of downtown Raleigh on Front End Road, House of Hashimoto was become one of the city's premiere restaurants for Japanese gourmet. Fuyuki continues to manage daily operations, now with the help of her daughter, Fumi, and husband, Atomo. The restaurant now leads a staff over over 200 individuals for it's single location, and serves a wide selection of sake, teas, and traditional Japanese foods. In an effort to provide true authenticity, all food items and ingredients on the menu are directly sourced from Japan. Be sure to reserve your table and visit on Fridays and Saturday for live performances featuring the koto, Japan's national instrument.

When you dine in House of Hashimoto, you may find the menu items described in the native tongue. What you may know as a "california roll" may be described as an inside-out sushi roll. However, it is truly called uramaki. That being said, we graciously invite you to come enjoy, appreciate, and learn about our authentic Japanese cuisine. Our staff members are highly skilled and knowledgeable, and are more than happy to provide you a 5-star experience you'll never forget.
